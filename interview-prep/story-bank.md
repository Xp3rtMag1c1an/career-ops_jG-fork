# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

This file accumulates your best interview stories over time. Each evaluation (Block F) adds new stories here. Instead of memorizing 100 answers, maintain 5-10 deep stories that you can bend to answer almost any behavioral question.

## How it works

1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

## Stories

### [GTM / New Market Entry] Avenue Alive: Zero-to-One Market Entry
**Source:** Report #002 — Tremendous — Special Projects Lead, GTM
**S (Situation):** Verizon FiOS/DirecTV needed to enter new direct sales markets with no existing playbook or team.
**T (Task):** Build market entry strategy, recruit and train reps, hit revenue targets from Day 1.
**A (Action):** Authored a national sales playbook from scratch, recruited 25+ reps, trained on objection handling, iterated the playbook weekly based on field data.
**R (Result):** $300K+ Y1 revenue, #1 national ranking from Day 1, playbook adopted by 400 reps across 40 markets.
**Reflection:** The playbook worked because I obsessed over the reps' actual objections, not theoretical ones. Scaling knowledge is harder than scaling headcount.
**Best for questions about:** GTM execution, scaling, new market entry, zero-to-one, leadership, playbook design

### [Competitive Intelligence] Findr AI: Competitive Intel to Product Roadmap
**Source:** Report #002 — Tremendous — Special Projects Lead, GTM
**S (Situation):** Findr AI needed to understand their competitive landscape to make product decisions.
**T (Task):** Map the competitive space and produce actionable recommendations that would directly influence the product roadmap.

### [Ambiguity / Ownership] Bridge2Rwanda: No Playbook, Ship Anyway
**Source:** Report #002 — Tremendous — Special Projects Lead, GTM
**S (Situation):** Arrived in Kigali, Rwanda with a vague mandate to develop curriculum and training — no existing materials, no precedent, no local team.
**T (Task):** Create usable curriculum and training frameworks from scratch in a completely unfamiliar context.
**A (Action):** Interviewed stakeholders, observed local learning patterns, built iteratively, aligned international and local priorities, shipped materials.
**R (Result):** Curriculum still in ongoing use by local leaders 15+ years later.
**Reflection:** The hardest part wasn't building the curriculum — it was listening long enough before building. In ambiguous situations, the first week should be pure discovery.
**Best for questions about:** ambiguity, ownership, cross-cultural work, building from scratch, adaptability

### [Systems / Throughput] Pulte Mortgage: Personal Operating Systems
**Source:** Report #002 — Tremendous — Special Projects Lead, GTM
**S (Situation):** Managing ~180 active loans with complex cross-functional dependencies (underwriting, title, appraisal, compliance).
**T (Task):** Maintain $63M annual volume with top-tier satisfaction while reducing personal error rate.
**A (Action):** Designed personal operating systems — checklists, escalation frameworks, communication templates — that systematized the entire pipeline.
**R (Result):** Top 3 of 150 consultants, 94% CSAT, consistent throughput without burnout.
**Reflection:** Systems beat willpower. The consultants who burned out were the ones trying to hold everything in their heads. I built the system once and iterated it.
**Best for questions about:** process improvement, high-volume operations, quality under pressure, systems thinking

### [Documentation / Solo Execution] writenode: Solo Founder Documentation Practice
**Source:** Report #002 — Tremendous — Special Projects Lead, GTM
**S (Situation):** Building a complex AI product solo — every architectural decision needed documentation for future reference and team onboarding.
**T (Task):** Maintain development velocity while creating comprehensive documentation.
**A (Action):** Built documentation-first workflow: architecture decisions recorded in real-time, classifier logic documented, cognitive loop specs written before code.
**R (Result):** Product is extensible and explainable — YC application, partnerships, and investor discussions all draw from this documentation.
**Reflection:** Writing forces clarity of thought. Every time I documented a design decision, I found at least one flaw in my reasoning. Documentation isn't overhead — it's a thinking tool.
**Best for questions about:** documentation culture, solo execution, technical communication, working independently
