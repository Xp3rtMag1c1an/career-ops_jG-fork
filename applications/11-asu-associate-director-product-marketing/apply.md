**"Why ASU Learning Enterprise?"**
> Lifelong learning and career upskilling is the right wedge for AI's impact on work — and I live 10 minutes from the Tempe campus, so I'm already part of this community. I bring three things that line up with this role specifically: (1) a national product/sales playbook I authored at Avenue Alive Direct adopted by 400 reps across 40 markets — product marketing at scale in its purest form, (2) active AI content strategy work at Harvest Investment Strategies where I built prompt engineering frameworks and LinkedIn funnel automation, and (3) writenode, the AI Chrome extension I built as solo founder, positioned around a distribution thesis (1.4B Chrome users vs 1M PKM users) that got it into a YC Spring 2026 application and a Hindsight/Vectorize partnership.

**"Tell us about a product marketing project you led."**
> At Avenue Alive Direct (Verizon FiOS / DirecTV) I authored a national sales and marketing playbook that was adopted by 400 reps across 40 markets. It wasn't a deck — it was positioning, messaging architecture, objection-handling framework, and launch sequencing all in one package. I was statistically #1 from Day One across multiple campaigns and multiple cities. Highest sales AND highest install retention in the company. The playbook worked because I built it bottom-up from field execution data — what reps actually heard at the door — not top-down from theory. That's exactly how I'd approach launching and scaling a Career and Professional Learning product: get positioning right, simplify the narrative until it's memorable, give the field trusted proof points, and make it so useful that adoption happens without a top-down mandate.

**"How do you think about AI in product marketing?"**
> I build with it. At Harvest Investment Strategies I'm currently running an AI-powered brand system for a wealth management firm reaching younger demographics: prompt engineering framework with LinkedIn funnel integration, digital persona system maintaining founder voice at scale, and content automation with measurable funnel impact. The design principle is "3 prompts, not 30" — because complexity kills adoption, especially with non-technical teams. For ASU Learning Enterprise, I'd apply the same instinct: use AI to compound the team's work without creating new dependencies or bottlenecks. AI should make the team's best work repeatable, not harder.

**"What's your experience with positioning and narrative?"**

(3) Through groves in the mist, my creative direction work with Arc'teryx, On Running, and Techstars Boulder founders has given me direct experience shaping how products show up visually and narratively — not just the pitch, but the whole story customers tell each other.
