**"How do you think about automation governance?"**
> Governance is where most automation rollouts die. Customers buy the magic, then hit the reality: who owns this Zap when the creator leaves, how do we audit changes, what happens when an upstream API changes shape, how do we sunset workflows that no one uses, how do we prevent shadow automation. I learned this at Pulte — a process only one person can run isn't a system, it's a dependency. Good governance means making automations teachable, observable, owned, and removable. It's not glamorous work, but it's the difference between an enterprise that says "we use Zapier" and an enterprise where automation is actually a strategic capability.
