**"Tell us about a complex deployment you led."**
> Avenue Alive Direct started in one market with one rep. By Year 3 it was operating across 40+ markets with 25+ direct hires and a national playbook adopted by 400 reps. The mechanics of scaling across distributed sites: (1) standardize the playbook from field execution data, not theory, (2) identify and empower local champions in each market, (3) instrument early signals so you can intervene before a site drifts, (4) make the playbook teachable so new sites can ramp without a senior trainer flying in. Same shape as deploying warehouse robots: standardized fleet behavior, local champion enablement, telemetry-driven intervention.

**"How do you handle a customer whose deployment is struggling?"**
> First, get out of the office. The data tells you something is wrong, but the data won't tell you why. At Pulte, when a loan was struggling I didn't escalate from my desk — I called the borrower, walked the situation, found the actual blocker. Same at Avenue Alive — when a market underperformed, I went to the market and rode along with reps. For warehouse robotics customers, it'd be a site visit (or video walkthrough) with the actual operators using the robots. Then: identify the gap, fix the immediate issue, then ask "what does this teach us about how we deploy the NEXT customer?" — turn one struggle into a process improvement that prevents the next ten.
